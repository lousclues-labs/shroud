The following uses are permitted without written authorization:

- **Descriptive reference** — Referring to the VPN Shroud project by name in articles, blog posts, documentation, or conversations (e.g., "compatible with VPN Shroud," "tested against VPN Shroud").
- **Attribution** — Stating that your project is "based on VPN Shroud" or "derived from VPN Shroud" when this is factually accurate.
- **Linking** — Linking to the official repository (https://github.com/lousclues-labs/shroud) or website (https://vpnshroud.org).

## Forks and Derivative Works

If you fork or create a derivative work from VPN Shroud's source code (as permitted by the GPL), you must:

1. **Choose a different name** for your project that does not include "VPN Shroud" or "lousclues."
2. **Remove or replace** any VPN Shroud or lousclues logos, icons, or branding from your fork.
3. **Clearly indicate** in your README and documentation that your project is a fork or derivative and is not the official VPN Shroud project.
4. **Include attribution** — State that your project is derived from VPN Shroud and link to the official repository.

**Acceptable examples:**
- ✅ "MyVPNManager — a fork of VPN Shroud"
- ✅ "VeilNet, based on VPN Shroud (https://github.com/lousclues-labs/shroud)"

**Unacceptable examples:**
- ❌ "VPN Shroud Enhanced"
- ❌ "VPN Shroud Community Edition"
- ❌ "VPN Shroud for Windows"
- ❌ "VPN Shroud 2.0"
