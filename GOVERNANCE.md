*Last updated: February 2026*

## Decision-Making

As a single-maintainer project, the decision-making process is straightforward:

- **Code changes:** All pull requests are reviewed and merged (or rejected) by the maintainer.
- **Releases:** The maintainer determines the release schedule and version numbering.
- **Licensing:** Only the maintainer can authorize changes to the project's licensing terms, grant commercial licenses, or modify the CLA.
- **Trademarks:** Only the maintainer can authorize use of the VPNShroud and lousclues trademarks.

## Succession Plan

### Why This Matters

All intellectual property, licensing authority, and decision-making power is concentrated in one individual. If the maintainer becomes permanently unavailable (due to incapacity, death, or extended absence), the project needs a clear path forward.

### Scenario 1: Extended Absence (Temporary)

If the maintainer is absent for an extended period but expected to return:

- The project continues under its existing licenses. The GPL ensures anyone can fork, modify, and distribute the code.
- No commercial licenses can be issued during the absence (only the maintainer has this authority).
- Open issues and pull requests will remain open until the maintainer returns.
- The community may maintain informal forks, which can be merged back when the maintainer returns.

### Scenario 2: Permanent Unavailability

If the maintainer becomes permanently unavailable:

**Open-Source License (GPL-3.0-or-later):**
- The GPL license is irrevocable. All existing releases remain available under GPL-3.0-or-later, permanently.
- Anyone may fork the project and continue development under the GPL (with a different name, per the [Trademark Policy](TRADEMARKS.md)).
- No action is needed to preserve open-source access — the GPL guarantees this.

**Commercial License:**
- No new commercial licenses can be issued without the maintainer's authority.
- Existing commercial license agreements remain valid per their terms.
- If a business entity (e.g., "lousclues LLC") is formed in the future, it would assume commercial licensing authority, and standard business succession rules would apply.

**Trademarks:**
- Trademark rights may lapse if not actively maintained (under U.S. common-law trademark rules). This means forks could eventually use the name "VPNShroud" if the mark is abandoned — but this process takes years, not days.
- The [Trademark Policy](TRADEMARKS.md) remains in effect unless formally revoked or the mark lapses.

### Scenario 3: Voluntary Transfer

If the maintainer chooses to step back voluntarily:

1. **Designate a successor** — The maintainer may designate a successor maintainer and transfer:
   - Repository admin access
   - Copyright (via written assignment)
   - Trademark ownership (via written assignment)
   - Commercial licensing authority
   - CLA management responsibilities

2. **Transfer to a foundation** — The maintainer may transfer the project to an open-source foundation (e.g., Software Freedom Conservancy, Apache Foundation, Linux Foundation) that would manage it going forward.

3. **Transfer to a business entity** — The maintainer may form or transfer the project to a business entity (e.g., "lousclues LLC") that assumes all IP, licensing, and governance responsibilities.

Any transfer would be documented publicly in the repository.

## Future Governance Evolution

As the project grows, governance may evolve. Possible future structures include:

- **Co-maintainer model** — Adding one or more co-maintainers with commit access and release authority (but not licensing or trademark authority, unless explicitly granted).
- **Business entity** — Forming "lousclues LLC" or similar entity to hold IP and manage commercial licensing.
- **Foundation stewardship** — Transferring the project to an established open-source foundation.

Any changes to governance will be documented in this file and announced in the CHANGELOG.
